# NDMC BSCS Thesis Guidelines & Research Agenda

This document compiles the approved thesis outline, formatting guidelines, and research agenda specifically for the **Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS)** program at Notre Dame of Midsayap College (NDMC), College of Information Technology and Engineering (CITE).

## 2. BSCS Proposed Thesis Outline
The official structure for the BSCS thesis manuscript consists of the following sections:

### Preliminary Pages
* Title Page
* Approval Sheet
* Certificate of Authentic Authorship
* Abstract (150-250 words, single paragraph; covers purpose, design, findings, conclusions, recommendations; 3-5 keywords)
* Acknowledgment
* Dedication
* Table of Contents (Title Case, dot leaders)
* List of Tables
* List of Figures

### Chapter 1: Introduction
* **1.1 Background of the Study** (Establish context, research gaps, global $\rightarrow$ national $\rightarrow$ local literature; **must be 2-3 pages long**)
* **1.2 Statement of the Problem** (Core research questions/objectives)
* **1.3 Significance of the Study** (Beneficiaries)
* **1.4 Scope and Delimitations of the Study** (Boundaries of research)
* **1.5 Definition of Terms** (Alphabetical; conceptual/lexical and operational definitions)
* **1.6 Review of Related Literature and Systems**
  * *1.6.1 Related Literature* (Synthesis and critical analysis of prior studies)
  * *1.6.2 Related Systems* (Review of existing software/technologies related to the study)
* **1.7 Theoretical Framework** (Theoretical anchors; maximum of 2 theories: 1 major, 1 minor)
* **1.8 Conceptual Framework** (Schematic diagram showing relationship of variables)
* **1.9 Hypothesis/es** (Stated in the null form, $H_0$, written in negative form)

### Chapter 2: Methodology
* **2.1 Research Design** (Explain and justify the chosen approach)
* **2.2 Locale and Respondents of the Study** (Setting and participants)
* **2.3 Sampling Techniques** (Sampling method used to select respondents)
* **2.4 Data Gathering Procedure** (Permits, administration, collection, management)
* **2.5 System Architecture/Algorithm Development**
  * *2.5.1 Context Diagram*
  * *2.5.2 Use Case Diagram*
  * *2.5.3 Architecture Diagram* (System structure, data flows, core algorithms)
* **2.6 Software and Tools** (Programming languages, frameworks, development tools used)
* **2.7 Data Collection**
* **2.8 Instrumentation** (Details on research tools, e.g., questionnaires or test scripts)
* **2.9 Validity and Reliability of the Instrument** (If researcher-made, must elaborate a 7-step development/pilot-testing process)
* **2.10 Statistical Tools and Treatment of Data** (Specific formulas, tools, and software utilized)
* **2.11 Ethical Considerations** (Informed consent, privacy, scientific integrity, referencing the Belmont Report of 1978)

### Chapter 3: Results and Discussion
* Presentation of data in tables or graphs (always introduce table first, highlight highest/lowest values, interpret and explain implications backed by RRL).
* Graphs must not duplicate tabular data.

### Chapter 4: Summary of Findings, Conclusions, and Recommendations
* **4.1 Summary of Findings** (Concise summary of results without interpretation)
* **4.2 Conclusions** (Generalizations addressing the problem statement)
* **4.3 Recommendations** (Actionable suggestions for future work)

### References
* Must follow **IEEE Citation** style guidelines (numeric citations, e.g., [1], [2]).

### Appendices
Depending on the research type (quantitative vs. qualitative), the appendices must be arranged as follows:

| Quantitative Research Appendices | Qualitative Research Appendices |
|---|---|
| **A** - Questionnaire / Instrument | **A** - Interview Guide |
| **B** - Transmittal Letters | **B** - Transmittal Letters |
| **C** - Informed Consent Form | **C** - Informed Consent Form |
| **D** - Validation Forms | **D** - Validation Forms |
| **E** - Reliability Results | **E** - Data Analysis Matrix |
| **F** - Drillbit Plagiarism Certificate | **F** - Drillbit Plagiarism Certificate |
| **G** - Grammarly Certificate | **G** - Grammarly Certificate |
| **H** - RDC-specific Appendices | **H** - RDC-specific Appendices |

* **Additional Appendices (CITE specific):** Evidence of User Testing/Deployment, Evidence of Data Gathering, Screen Layouts, and User Manual.

### Curriculum Vitae
* 1-page CV with formal 2x2 photo. Do NOT include sensitive personal data (e.g., mobile number, home address).

## 3. Thesis Layout & Formatting Rules
* **Paper Size:** **A4**
* **Paper Margins:** Left: **1.50 in** (for binding), Right: **1.0 in**, Top: **1.20 in**, Bottom: **1.0 in**.
* **Spacing & Headings:**
  * Double-space standard body text.
  * Headings (e.g., **Chapter 1**) are centered and bold.
  * Use **3 single spaces** between:
    * The top margin and Chapter title.
    * The Chapter title and the Section title (e.g., Introduction $\rightarrow$ Background of the Study).
    * Section title and the first line of the body paragraph.
* **Citations & Tense Usage:**
  * Must use **IEEE Citation** format (e.g., *"...as shown in [1]"* or *"...according to Loberiza [2]"*).
  * When referencing specific research findings, use the **past tense** (e.g., *"...tested the system [1]"*).
  * When discussing general concepts, theories, and ongoing results, use the **present tense** (e.g., *"...argues that [3]"*).
  * Direct quotes $\le$ 3 lines ($<$ 40 words) are integrated in the text, introduced with a colon, and enclosed in quotation marks.
  * Direct quotes $>$ 3 lines are indented, single-spaced, and do not use quotation marks.
* **Tables & Figures:** Number systematically in Arabic numerals (e.g., **Table 2.1**, **Figure 1.1**). Use single-spacing for titles.

## 4. Hard Bound Casing Color & Spine Design
* **Casing Color:** **Gray** (assigned color for CITE).
* **Spine Design:**
  * Left: Abbreviation of the College/Unit (**CITE**) in 1-inch block.
  * Center: Title of the thesis in **ALL CAPS**.
  * Right: Month and Year of publication (2-inch block).
  * Top and bottom edges bordered by **two gold lines**.
